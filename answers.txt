The first humans created by God were Adam and Eve.
Adam and Eve were forbidden to eat from the tree of the knowledge of good and evil.
The serpent tempted Eve to eat the forbidden fruit.
Cain's offering was not favorable to God, unlike his brother Abel's.
Cain was marked by God and became a wanderer on the earth.
God saw how great the wickedness of the human race had become and decided to wipe out humanity, except for Noah and his family.
The floodwaters remained on the earth for 150 days.
Noah's Ark came to rest upon the mountains of Ararat.
The rainbow was the sign of the covenant between God and all living creatures.
God confused the language of the people, making them unable to understand each other, and scattered them over the face of the earth.
God called Abram to go to the land of Canaan.
Abram was renamed Abraham, and Sarai was renamed Sarah.
Isaac was the son born to Abraham and Sarah.
Sodom and Gomorrah were destroyed because of their wickedness.
Isaac's wife was Rebekah.
Esau and Jacob were the twin sons of Isaac and Rebekah.
Esau traded his birthright to Jacob for a bowl of stew.
Jacob fled because he feared Esau's anger after deceiving their father, Isaac, and stealing Esau's blessing.
Jacob's two wives were Leah and Rachel.
Joseph was sold into slavery by his jealous brothers and was taken to Egypt.
